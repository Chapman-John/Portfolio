Books worth reading

Books I've recently read and enjoyed:

The Beginning of Infinity: Explanations that Transform the World

By David Deutsch

He brilliantly describes how explanatory knowledge has been the best way for us to make progress. Key points: All knowledge is conjectural and needs to be open to criticism. Problems are inevitable, yet all problems are soluble.

Straw Dogs: Thoughts on Humans and Other Animals

By John Gray

Read the first five pages, and it will send most Westerner's worldview in question. In the book, he reminds us not to forget human behavior when it comes to progress, especially progress outside of science.

Antifragile: Things That Gain From Disorder

By Nassim Nicholas Taleb

He employs a powerful concept: something that becomes stronger when stressed, rather than breaking—antifragility. Time is more knowledgeable about fragilities and breaks them when necessary.
